The artist

Ebtisam painter & botanist at heart

I've always been drawn to the moment just before a leaf catches the light — that suspended stillness where colour and shadow decide what to do next. Painting is how I slow down long enough to really look.

My work focuses on botanical subjects: branches in winter and in bloom, single leaves held against the sky, garden corners that most people walk past. I work primarily in acrylic, building up thin washes to find the luminosity I'm after.

Each painting is made at home, usually in the early morning, when the light is still deciding what it wants to be.
